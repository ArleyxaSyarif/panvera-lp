High Financial Burden (CapEx Trap)

Many traditional IT vendors require large capital expenditures (CapEx) upfront before the system has proven its real value to your business. This often drains cash flow and creates high financial risk if the project does not go as expected.

The “Launch and Leave” Phenomenon

Often, once software development is complete and the vendor has been paid, the system is abandoned without maintenance. Without ongoing support, technology quickly becomes obsolete, vulnerable to security threats, and ultimately becomes useless digital waste in a short period of time.

Strategic Misalignment

There is often a significant gap between technical delivery and the strategic goals of an organization. Much software is built without a deep understanding of business processes, failing to solve specific problems such as workflow efficiency or accreditation compliance reporting.

Non-Scalable Systems

Many systems are built without a solid foundation, so when the business starts to grow, the technology architecture is unable to handle the surge in data or users. As a result, companies have to undergo a total overhaul, which will incur huge costs again in the future.
